# Mayfair Salon Homepage Design

**Date:** 2026-04-21

## Goal

Redesign the `Editorial` landing page so it feels like a luxury editorial salon with a Mayfair London sensibility, while keeping the publication structure and published essay URLs unchanged.

## Positioning

The homepage should feel like a private editorial salon first and a public website second. It should communicate confidence, restraint, and taste through layout, typography, imagery, and pacing rather than through promotional language.

## Visual Direction

The current homepage is readable but too generic and card-based. The redesign should introduce:

- dark, evening-toned foundations
- warm stone, parchment, and brass accents
- stronger editorial typography and whitespace
- less UI-card energy and more composed publication rhythm
- discreet luxury rather than loud brand theatrics

The tone should feel closer to a private Mayfair reading room or salon than a startup landing page.

## Structure

### 1. Hero / Cover

A more atmospheric editorial cover with:

- restrained masthead
- stronger headline presentation
- featured-essay pathway
- background imagery or layered visual texture

### 2. Featured Essay

The current featured piece remains `The Future of Travel 2026`, but it should be staged with more visual gravity and less boxed-card treatment.

### 3. Curated Archive

The archive should present essays as collected editorial entries rather than product cards. The two current essays remain linked:

- `/essays/the-future-of-travel-2026.html`
- `/essays/thirty-minutes-of-truth.html`

### 4. Editorial / Advisory Note

This should read as a salon note or private editorial brief. Advisory presence remains, but discreetly.
